Origin of Ewa Agoyin

Agoyin is a phrase coined by Nigerians to refer to Beninese and Togolese people. Ewa Agoyin is derived from Ewa which means Beans in Yoruba, and Agoyin which is a tribe in the Benin Republic or a term used to refer to the Benineseand Togolese people'
It was first introduced into Nigerian cuisine in the 1960s by the first generation of Agoyin migrantsto the country. It grew popularity in Lagos throughout the 1980s, and by the mid-nineties, it has swept across Africa like wildfire

Ewa Agoyin Recipe

3 cups of beans
100g of Dried chilli peppers
2 cups of Palm oil
2 big onions
some sliced onions
crayfish (optional)
salt and seasoning

Preparation

Pick your beans and ensure it's dirt free
Perboil your beans for about 8mins
Drain and rinse
Pour beans back into your pot, add enough water and leave to boil
Once the beans is getting dry,add little water to stop the beans from burning. do this continously until your beans is very soft.
Add salt to taste at the tail of your cooking just before it gets completely soft.
Use a wooden spoon to mash your beans until it becomes someting like this:

The Sauce

Soak your dried chilli peppers in hot for about 2 hours
Blend together with the 2 big onions and crayfish (the onions help to tone down the hotness)
Heat up your palm oil (you'll need lots of it)
Add your sliced onion and fry until its golden brown, this will aid the crunchiness of the sauce
Pour in your grinded pepper and fry until the sause starts to get dark
Add salt and seasoning to taste
Then fry until your sauce finally becomes dried and dark in color.
Our Delicious Ewa Agoyin is Ready!!!. Serve with fried plantain my favourite Agege Bread
